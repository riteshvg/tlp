What is a journey in AJO?

What Is a Journey in Adobe Journey Optimizer?

A journey is a multi-step orchestration that allows you to design and execute real-time, personalized customer experiences across multiple channels. Journeys combine events, orchestration activities, actions, and messages to create contextual experiences based on customer behavior and business events.

Key Characteristics

Real-time & Event-driven — Journeys react immediately to customer actions or business events rather than following a fixed schedule.

1:1 Personalization — Each profile travels their own path at their own pace. AJO tracks where each person is and reacts to their specific behavior and profile data.

Multi-channel orchestration — Send messages across email, SMS, push, in-app, web, code-based experiences, content cards, and more—all within a single workflow.

Contextual logic — Use conditions, wait times, and branching to create sophisticated flows that adapt based on customer data, events, and business rules.

Persistent & intelligent — Journeys maintain state across touchpoints and can include frequency capping, re-entrance rules, and audience qualification triggers.

How Journeys Work in Practice

Here's a simplified example of a cart abandonment journey:

```

Customer A: Abandoned cart → Wait 2 hours → No purchase? → Send reminder → Purchased? → End

Customer B: Abandoned cart → Wait 2 hours → Already purchased → End immediately

```

Each customer experiences a different timeline based on what they actually do. Customer A receives a reminder because they haven't purchased; Customer B skips it because they already bought.

Four Types of Journeys

AJO supports four journey types, each triggered differently:

1. Unitary Event Journeys — Triggered individually when a customer performs a specific action (e.g., abandons cart, signs up, makes a purchase). Profiles enter one at a time as the event occurs.

2. Read Audience Journeys — Start with an audience from Adobe Experience Platform and send messages in batch to all profiles in that audience at a scheduled time.

3. Audience Qualification Journeys — Triggered when profiles qualify for (or exit from) a specific audience segment. Profiles enter as they meet the audience criteria in real time.

4. Business Event Journeys — Triggered by business events (e.g., stock updates, weather alerts, price changes) that affect multiple profiles simultaneously.

Journey vs. Campaign

Journeys are best for multi-step, real-time experiences with conditional logic where each profile moves through at their own pace (1:1 orchestration).

Campaigns are better for scheduled or recurring activations to audiences processed together in batch, or event-driven/transactional messages triggered via API.
