When should I use last touch attribution versus other models for different marketing channels?

When to Use Last Touch Attribution vs. Other Models

What the documentation confirms:

Last Touch attribution gives 100% credit to the most recent touchpoint before conversion. The documentation states it is "the most basic and common attribution model" and is "frequently used for conversions with a short consideration cycle." It is commonly used by teams managing search marketing or analyzing internal search keywords.

The context also documents several alternative models with specific use cases:

  • First Touch: Useful for analyzing channels intended to drive brand awareness or customer acquisition; frequently used by display or social marketing teams.
  • Linear: Useful for conversions with longer consideration cycles or experiences requiring frequent customer engagement; often used for mobile app notifications or subscription products.
  • Same Touch: Helpful when evaluating content or user experience presented at the moment of conversion; used by product or design teams.
  • U-Shaped: Gives 40% credit to first and last interactions, 20% to middle touchpoints; useful for conversions with multiple touchpoints.

What this does NOT cover:

The documentation does not provide detailed channel-by-channel prescriptive guidance (e.g., which specific model to use for paid search vs. affiliates vs. email). It does not explain how to evaluate your specific business context, customer journey length, or organizational priorities to select a model.
