In Facebook Ads Manager

After activation, your audiences appear in Facebook Ads Manager as Custom Audiences. Here's how you interact with them:

Key Points:

  • Audience Creation: Your audiences from AEP are created as Facebook Custom Audiences
  • Placement Selection: In Facebook Ads Manager, you select which of Facebook's apps to target:
  • Facebook feed
  • Instagram feed
  • Audience Network
  • Messenger

This selection happens at the placement level within Facebook Ads Manager — not during the AEP activation process

  • Targeting & Personalization: Once in Facebook, you can use these audiences for:
  • Audience targeting — reach specific customer segments
  • Personalization — show tailored ads and offers
  • Suppression — exclude certain audiences from campaigns

Real-World Use Cases

Example 1: Personalized Offers to Existing Customers

An online retailer sends hashed email addresses from their CRM to AEP, builds an audience of past purchasers, and activates it to Facebook. In Ads Manager, they create a campaign targeting this audience with personalized product recommendations on Instagram and Facebook.

Example 2: Suppression/Exclusion

A company activates a "recent purchasers" audience to Facebook and uses it as an exclusion list in their ads to avoid showing product ads to people who already bought within the last 30 days.

Important Note

The specific visual appearance and UI of these audiences in Facebook Ads Manager depends on Facebook's interface and is outside the scope of AEP documentation. However, they will appear as standard Custom Audiences alongside any other audiences you've created directly in Facebook.
