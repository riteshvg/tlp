How does identity resolution work in Adobe Experience Platform?

Identity Resolution in Adobe Experience Platform

Overview

Adobe Experience Platform Identity Service provides a comprehensive view of your customers and their behavior by bridging identities across devices and systems. This allows you to deliver impactful, personalized digital experiences in real time.

Core Concept: The Identity Graph

At the heart of identity resolution in AEP is the identity graph — a map of relationships between different identities belonging to the same individual. Each graph links identities across devices, channels, and systems (e.g., CRM IDs, email addresses, cookie IDs, mobile device IDs) into a unified customer profile.

How It Works

1. Data Ingestion — Customer experience data is collected from client-side and server-side sources and sent to the Adobe Experience Platform Edge Network.

2. Identity Linking — As data arrives, Identity Service detects and links known identifiers (e.g., a logged-in CRM ID) with anonymous identifiers (e.g., a cookie or device ID), stitching them together in the identity graph.

3. Graph Updates — Each time new identity data is ingested, the graph is updated to reflect the latest known relationships between identifiers.

4. Profile Enrichment — The unified identity graph powers the Real-Time Customer Profile, enabling a single, merged view of the customer across all touchpoints.

Identity Types & Namespaces

Identities are organized using identity namespaces, which provide context for what a given identifier represents. Key distinctions include:

  • Cross-device identity types — e.g., CRM IDs, email addresses (tied to a person)
  • Cookie/device identity types — e.g., ECID, cookie IDs (tied to a device or browser)

> ⚠️ Important: Configuring person-level identifiers (like CRM IDs) as cookie/device identity types — or vice versa — can cause unintended graph behavior. Adobe Engineering must manually correct such misconfigurations.

Identity Graph Limits (Guardrails)

AEP enforces guardrails to maintain graph accuracy and performance:

| Guardrail | Detail |

|---|---|

| Identities per graph | Limit of 50 identities per graph (updated limit) |

| Eviction policy | When the limit is reached, the oldest identity is deleted based on ingestion timestamp and identity type |

| Eviction priority | Cookie identity types are deleted first |

Previously, the limit was 150 identities per graph; once reached, graphs were no longer updated. The updated 50-identity limit ensures graphs remain current by evicting stale identities.

Key Benefits

  • Real-time stitching — Identities are linked as data is ingested, keeping profiles current
  • Cross-device recognition — Connects anonymous browsing behavior to known customer profiles upon authentication
  • Personalization at scale — The resolved identity graph powers audience segmentation, activation, and real-time personalization across Adobe and non-Adobe destinations

Important Configuration Consideration

If your production sandbox contains:

  • A custom namespace where person identifiers (e.g., CRM IDs) are configured as cookie/device identity type, or
  • A custom namespace where cookie/device identifiers are configured as cross-device identity type

You should contact your Adobe account representative to request a corrective identity type change, as this requires manual processing by Adobe Engineering.
