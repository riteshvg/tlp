Can you walk me through creating a journey KPI and how it differs from standard email engagement metrics?

Journey KPIs vs. Standard Email Engagement Metrics in Adobe Journey Optimizer

Great question — understanding this distinction is key to measuring journey success effectively. Let me walk you through both.

What Makes a Good KPI?

Before defining KPIs, ask yourself: "If my executive could only know 5 things about business health, what would they be?"

A metric like average time on page tells a leader very little. But average revenue per visit = $2.00 across 2M visits immediately signals business health. The same principle applies to journeys — your KPIs should reflect business outcomes, not just activity.

How to Define Journey KPIs

Use a framework like this to map business objectives to measurable signals:

| Business Objective | Example Metrics |

|---|---|

| Increase sales through digital channels | Revenue per visit, conversion rate |

| Increase brand awareness | Unique visitors, reach |

| Drive deeper customer relationships | Logins, CTA clicks, repeat engagement |

| Site/journey conversion | CTA clicks / Total entries |

| Customer engagement | Messages interacted with, clicks per journey step |

> Tip: Refresh your KPIs at least every 6 months — business priorities shift frequently.

Standard Email Engagement Metrics in AJO

In Adobe Journey Optimizer, email campaign and journey reports surface metrics such as:

  • Email KPIs — delivered, opens, clicks, bounces, unsubscribes
  • Click and delivery funnels — tracking drop-off across the delivery pipeline
  • Tracking statistics — link clicks, tracked opens
  • Bounce and error reasons — hard vs. soft bounces, delivery failures
  • Engagement by domain, subject, and link — granular breakdowns of recipient behavior

> ⚠️ Important caveat on open rates: Apple's Mail Privacy Protection (MPP) has significantly impacted the reliability of email open tracking pixels. AJO's documentation explicitly recommends focusing on clicks and conversion metrics rather than open rates for more accurate insights.

Key Differences: Journey KPIs vs. Email Engagement Metrics

| Dimension | Journey KPIs | Standard Email Engagement Metrics |

|---|---|---|

| Scope | Business outcome-oriented (revenue, relationships, conversion) | Channel activity-oriented (opens, clicks, bounces) |

| Purpose | Answer "Is the journey achieving business goals?" | Answer "How did this email perform technically?" |

| Audience | Executive / strategic stakeholders | Marketing operations / campaign managers |

| Refresh cadence | Every 6 months minimum | Per send / campaign |

| Reliability concern | Lower — tied to downstream outcomes | Higher risk — open rates impacted by Apple MPP |

Practical Recommendation

When building journey success measurement in AJO:

1. Start with your business objective (e.g., re-engage lapsed customers)

2. Map it to a journey outcome (e.g., purchase within 7 days of journey entry)

3. Use email clicks and conversions — not opens — as your engagement signal

4. Access journey/campaign reports via the Reports button → View all time report in the AJO UI

5. Revisit and refine your KPI definitions regularly as business needs evolve

This approach ensures your journey measurement tells a meaningful story rather than simply counting email activity.
