What are the differences between first-touch and last-touch attribution models for marketing channels in CJA?

First-Touch vs. Last-Touch Attribution Models in Customer Journey Analytics

Core Definitions

| Attribution Model | Credit Assignment | Example |

| --- | --- | --- |

| First Touch | Gives 100% credit to the first touchpoint seen in the attribution lookback window | If a customer visits via organic search, then clicks a paid ad, then converts — organic search gets 100% credit |

| Last Touch | Gives 100% credit to the most recent touchpoint before conversion | Same customer journey as above — the paid ad gets 100% credit |

When to Use Each Model

First-Touch Attribution

Best for:

  • Brand awareness and customer acquisition campaigns: Understanding which channels first introduce customers to your brand
  • Display and social marketing teams: Evaluating top-of-funnel effectiveness
  • Product recommendations: Assessing the effectiveness of initial engagement drivers
  • Longer consideration cycles: Where initial exposure plays a crucial role in eventual conversion

Key insight: Answers the question "Which channel got this customer's attention first?"

Last-Touch Attribution

Best for:

  • Conversions with short consideration cycles: When customers decide and act quickly
  • Search marketing teams: Understanding which keywords or channels directly preceded the purchase
  • Internal search optimization: Analyzing what customers searched for immediately before converting
  • Direct response campaigns: Evaluating channels that close the sale

Key insight: Answers the question "Which channel closed the deal?"

Practical Example in CJA

Here's how you'd apply these models in Analysis Workspace:

1. Drag the Marketing Channel dimension into a Freeform table

2. Right-click a metric column header and select Use non-default attribution model

3. Choose either First Touch or Last Touch with an appropriate lookback window

You can also use the Attribution panel for a side-by-side comparison of both models' performance on the same channels.

Why Both Matter

The context shows a real-world example where Google Ads drove both online sales and mobile app revenue. Using First Touch reveals that online ads initiated interest in the mobile app (showing $14k-$15k in indirect influence), while Last Touch would only credit the mobile channel where the actual purchase occurred. Together, these models give you the complete picture of your marketing funnel.

Advanced Flexibility in CJA

Unlike traditional BI tools, Customer Journey Analytics lets you:

  • Compare unlimited attribution models dynamically — test as many models as needed
  • Avoid implementation changes — adjust attribution at report time without modifying data collection
  • Apply attribution to any dimension — not just marketing campaigns, but also internal search, product categories, or any custom dimension
  • Segment by important groupings — compare first-touch vs. last-touch performance across New vs. Repeat customers, Product X vs. Product Y, or loyalty levels

This flexibility enables you to match your attribution logic to your actual business decision-making process.
