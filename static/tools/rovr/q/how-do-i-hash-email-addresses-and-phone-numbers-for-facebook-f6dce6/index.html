How do I hash email addresses and phone numbers for Facebook audience activation?

Hashing Email Addresses & Phone Numbers for Facebook Audience Activation

To achieve the highest audience match rates in Facebook, it is strongly recommended to use the `email_lc_sha256` and `phone_sha256` target identities, as these are the primary identifiers Facebook uses for matching audiences across their platforms.

Email Address Hashing

You have two options for handling email addresses:

Option 1: Ingest Raw Emails (Platform Hashes Automatically)

  • Ingest raw email addresses into Experience Platform
  • AEP will automatically hash them upon activation
  • No manual hashing required

Option 2: Pre-Hash Emails Before Ingestion

If you choose to hash emails yourself before ingesting into AEP, you must comply with the following normalization requirements:

Step-by-Step Email Normalization Rules

1. Remove leading and trailing spaces from the email address

2. Convert all ASCII characters to lowercase

3. For `gmail.com` addresses specifically, also remove the following from the username part:

  • The period (`.`) character (ASCII code 46)
  • e.g., normalize `jane.doe@gmail.com` → `janedoe@gmail.com`
  • The plus sign (`+`) character (ASCII code 43) and all characters after it
  • e.g., normalize `janedoe+home@gmail.com` → `janedoe@gmail.com`

4. After normalization, apply SHA-256 hashing to the resulting string

5. Always ingest pre-hashed emails into the `Email_LC_SHA256` namespace in AEP

Phone Number Hashing

There are two methods to activate phone numbers in Facebook:

Method 1: Ingest Raw Phone Numbers (Platform Hashes Automatically)

  • Ingest raw phone numbers in E.164 format into Experience Platform
  • E.164 format example: `+14155552671`
  • AEP will automatically hash them upon activation
  • Always ingest raw phone numbers into the `Phone_E.164` namespace

Method 2: Pre-Hash Phone Numbers Before Ingestion

  • Hash your phone numbers before ingesting into AEP
  • Apply SHA-256 hashing after proper normalization
  • Always ingest pre-hashed phone numbers into the `Phone_SHA256` namespace

> ⚠️ Important: Phone numbers ingested into the generic `Phone` namespace cannot be activated in Facebook. You must use either `Phone_E.164` (raw) or `Phone_SHA256` (pre-hashed).

Summary Table

| Data Type | Raw Ingestion Namespace | Pre-Hashed Namespace | Hashed By |

|---|---|---|---|

| Email | (ingest raw, auto-hashed) | `Email_LC_SHA256` | You (pre-ingestion) |

| Phone Number | `Phone_E.164` | `Phone_SHA256` | You (pre-ingestion) |

Identity Mapping in the Activation Workflow

When you reach the Mapping step during audience activation, map your source identity fields to the correct Facebook target identities:

  • Source: Your profile attribute or identity namespace (e.g., `Email_LC_SHA256`)
  • Target: Facebook's expected identity (e.g., `email_lc_sha256`)

Ensuring your data is correctly normalized, hashed, and mapped to the right namespaces is the single most important factor in achieving high match rates on Facebook Custom Audiences.
