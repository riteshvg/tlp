What the Documentation Does Confirm

Attribution Model Flexibility with Marketing Channels

CJA supports applying any attribution model to your Marketing Channel dimension:

  • Generic Marketing Channel and Marketing Channel Detail dimensions work with your desired attribution model at report time
  • You can use unlimited attribution model comparison — dynamically test multiple models against the same channels
  • Attribution models available include: First Touch, Last Touch, Linear, Time Decay, U-Shaped, Inverse J, Position-Based, and Algorithmic

How to Compare Models Across Channels

The documentation confirms you should:

1. Do exploratory analysis first — Understand your customer behavior before picking an attribution model:

  • How many customers touch different marketing channels before converting?
  • What is the proportion/distribution of these behaviors?
  • Is there interaction among multiple channels?

2. Break down attribution by segments — Compare channel performance across important customer groupings:

  • New vs. Repeat customers
  • Product X vs. Product Y
  • Loyalty level or CLV

3. Analyze marketing sequences visually — Use flow and fallout visualizations to explore paths that led to conversion

Recommended Next Steps

To determine the best attribution model for your specific channel mix, Adobe's best practices guidance suggests:

1. Start with exploratory analysis of your customer journey data

2. Test multiple models (one advantage of CJA is you can compare unlimited models simultaneously)

3. Match the model to your business question:

  • If you want to understand first engagement → use First Touch
  • If you want to understand what closed the sale → use Last Touch
  • If you want to account for multi-touch influence → use Linear, Time Decay, U-Shaped, or Algorithmic

For channel-specific recommendations beyond what's documented here, I'd suggest consulting Adobe's professional services team or exploring your CJA documentation's full Attribution section, as those resources may contain more detailed channel-specific guidance.
