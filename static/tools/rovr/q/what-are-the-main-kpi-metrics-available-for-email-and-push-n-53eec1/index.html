What are the main KPI metrics available for email and push notification channels in Adobe Journey Optimizer?

Main KPI Metrics for Email and Push Notifications in Adobe Journey Optimizer

Email Channel KPIs

Core Email Metrics

+++ Click to expand Email KPI metrics

  • [!UICONTROL Click through rate]: Percentage of users who interacted with the message.
  • [!UICONTROL Clicks]: Number of times a content was clicked on in your message.
  • [!UICONTROL Delivered]: Number of emails successfully sent, in relation to the total number of sent messages.
  • [!UICONTROL Displays]: Number of times the message was opened.
  • [!UICONTROL Unique Click-through rate]: Percentage of unique profiles who clicked on at least one link in the email, relative to the number of unique delivered emails.
  • [!UICONTROL Click through open rate (CTOR)]: Percentage of profiles who interacted with the message.
  • [!UICONTROL Unique Open rate]: Percentage of unique profiles who opened the email at least once, relative to the number of unique delivered emails.
  • [!UICONTROL Unique Bounce rate]: Percentage of unique profiles whose email bounced at least once, based on the total number of unique sends.
  • [!UICONTROL Unique delivered]: Number of unique profiles that successfully received at least one message.
  • [!UICONTROL Estimated Opens]: Estimate of total email opens that accounts for both direct opens by profiles and automated opens triggered by mail servers. This metric adjusts for opens triggered by mail servers for privacy or security scanning.
  • [!UICONTROL Unique Estimated Opens]: Estimate of the number of unique email recipients who likely opened the email.

+++

Push Notification Channel KPIs

Push Notification Sending Statistics

Based on the Journey Report for Push Notifications, the following sending statistics are tracked:

+++ Click to expand Push Notification KPI metrics

  • [!UICONTROL Sent]: Total number of push notifications sent.
  • [!UICONTROL Delivered]: Number of push notifications successfully delivered to user devices.
  • [!UICONTROL Bounces]: Total number of push notifications that failed to deliver (both hard and soft bounces).
  • [!UICONTROL Errors]: Total number of errors that occurred during the sending process, preventing delivery.
  • [!UICONTROL Excluded]: Number of profiles excluded from receiving the push notification due to:
  • Missing required attributes
  • Consent/opt-out status
  • Audience eligibility criteria
  • [!UICONTROL Opens]: Number of times users opened/engaged with the push notification.
  • [!UICONTROL Clicks]: Number of times users clicked on links or call-to-action buttons within the push notification.
  • [!UICONTROL Unique Opens]: Number of unique profiles who opened the push notification at least once.
  • [!UICONTROL Unique Clicks]: Number of unique profiles who clicked on content in the push notification at least once.
  • [!UICONTROL Click-through Rate (CTR)]: Percentage of users who clicked on the push notification relative to those who received it.
  • [!UICONTROL Open Rate]: Percentage of delivered push notifications that were opened by users.

+++

Tracked Link Metrics (Both Email & Push)

For both email and push notifications, you can also track:

  • [!UICONTROL Tracked link labels and URLs]: Details of which specific links within your messages were clicked
  • [!UICONTROL Click-through by link]: Individual performance metrics for each unique link in your message

Bounce & Error Reasons (Both Channels)

Email Bounce Reasons

  • Soft bounces (temporary delivery issues)
  • Hard bounces (invalid addresses, domain issues)
  • Spam complaints

Push Notification Error & Bounce Reasons

  • Invalid device tokens
  • Device uninstalled the app
  • User opted out of notifications
  • Platform errors (iOS, Android)

Exclusion Reasons (Both Channels)

  • Missing required attributes
  • Consent not provided
  • Profile not in target audience
  • Frequency/capping rules applied

How to Access These KPIs

For Journeys

1. Navigate to your Journey

2. Click the [!UICONTROL View report] button

3. Select the Email or Push notification channel section

4. Review the Sending Statistics, Tracked Links, and Bounce/Error Reasons sections

For Campaigns

1. Navigate to your Campaign

2. Open the Campaign KPIs dashboard

3. View Email or Push notification specific metrics

4. Review engagement and delivery performance

Best Practices for Monitoring Email & Push KPIs

1. Set baseline expectations for open rates, click rates, and bounce rates based on industry standards and your historical performance

2. Monitor trends over time — track how metrics evolve across campaigns

3. Segment by audience — compare KPI performance across different customer segments

4. A/B test elements — use KPIs to determine winning subject lines, send times, and CTAs

5. Track bounce & error reasons — address recurring issues (e.g., invalid addresses, opt-out status)

6. Optimize exclusion rates — reduce unnecessary exclusions by ensuring data quality and attribute completeness
