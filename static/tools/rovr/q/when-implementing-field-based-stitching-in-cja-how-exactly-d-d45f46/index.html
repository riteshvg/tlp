Field-Based Stitching in CJA: Lookback Windows & Metric Impact

How the Lookback Window Works

The retrieved documentation confirms that field-based stitching exists and operates in CJA, but does not contain the specific technical detail about how timestamp collisions are resolved between anonymous and authenticated profiles within a 7-day or 14-day lookback window. I can tell you what the documentation does support:

Field-based stitching creates a separate stitched dataset in addition to the source dataset. The stitching process merges authenticated and unauthenticated user data — and the documentation explicitly calls out compliance obligations around this merge:

> "Stitching includes merging authenticated and unauthenticated user data. Ensure that you comply with applicable laws and regulations, including obtaining necessary end-user permissions, before activating stitching on an event dataset."

What Happens to Historical Metrics After Stitching

The documentation does confirm the following downstream effects:

  • Stitched datasets are separate from the original — your original unstitched dataset is preserved, so you can compare pre- and post-stitching behavior
  • Session definitions, attribution logic, and unique visitor-equivalent metrics in CJA are controlled at the Data View level, not in the raw dataset — meaning stitching affects the person IDs in the dataset, and those revised IDs flow into however your connection and data view define sessions and people counts
  • Unique visitor counts will change — anonymous events that are backfilled with an authenticated person ID will collapse what were previously counted as separate "people" into a single identified person, reducing unique person counts and potentially altering session counts depending on your session definition settings

Validating the Impact

The documentation references a dedicated "Validate Stitching" topic specifically for measuring authentication rates and identification before and after stitching — this is the recommended approach to quantify the metric delta in your reporting before committing to stitched datasets in production connections.
